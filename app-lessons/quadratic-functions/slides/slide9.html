9.) Consider the following

$$h(x)$$ is a linear function. Its graph is a straight line. For instance $$h(x) = 3x+1$$

As we know, to obtain the graph of $$f(x)$$ we need, for each $$x$$, to add values of $$g(x)$$ and $$h(x)$$. The graph of $$g(x)$$ is a nice symmetric parabola. But the line $$h(x)$$ is angled up, so as we trend to the right in $$x$$, we add bigger and bigger values to the $$g(x)$$, and that may affect the shape of parabola. But how?

Does it still look like a parabola? Is it still symmetric? Is it wider or narrower than $$g(x) = ax^2$$? Do the branches extend up or down?
